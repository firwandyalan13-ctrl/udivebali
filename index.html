Course Description

The PADI Open Water Course is the most popular introductory certification for scuba divers. Upon completing this course, you will receive your PADI Open Water Diver Certification and be able to dive to a maximum depth of 18 meters/60 feet.

Knowledge Development: Through reading, exercises, quizzes, and instructor-led training, you'll learn the essential principles to safely enjoy scuba diving.

Confined Open Water Dive: Your first dive takes place in the pool — your instructor will guide you through the skills in practice.

4 Open Water Dives: Head out to Tulamben's best dive sites to perfect your skills and explore the fascinating marine environment.

Final Exam: Students must pass a written exam to receive their Open Water Certification.

Course Description

Build upon the basic scuba diving knowledge you learned in your PADI Open Water Diver Course. Upon completing this course, you will receive your PADI Advanced Open Water Diver Certification and be able to dive to a maximum depth of 30 meters/100 feet.

Knowledge Development: Through a combination of reading, short exercises, quizzes, and instructor-led training, you'll learn more specialized skills and techniques related to each of the Adventure Dives you select.

Five Adventure Dives: All students are required to complete one Deep Dive and one Underwater Navigation Dive. In addition, you'll complete three Adventure Dives of your choice. You can credit these toward the related PADI specialty course.

Available Adventure Dives: Altitude · Boat · Digital Underwater Photography · Diver Propulsion Vehicle · Drift · Dry Suit · Enriched Air · Fish Identification · Multilevel · Night · Peak Performance Buoyancy · Public Safety · Search and Recovery · Underwater Naturalist · Underwater Videography · Wreck

Description

If you are new to scuba diving or are not a certified diver, then this is the option for you. Prior to gearing up, your dive master will provide you with a briefing on basic dive techniques such as mask clearing, BCD and regulator use, hand signals, and safety principles. If it's your first time diving or if you would simply like to refresh your skills, you will also get a chance to practice these techniques in a pool or water with pool-like conditions prior to your dive. Each beginner diver will be accompanied by their own dive master during the entire dive. Since your first dive will be mainly focused on getting you comfortable using the gear underwater, we highly recommended doing a second dive so that you can fully appreciate and enjoy the unique experience of diving. (This is also known as the PADI Discover Scuba Diving Program).
